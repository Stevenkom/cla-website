About

Kenneth Onyemna Amadi is a Ph.D. candidate in Theology majoring in Liturgical Studies. With previous degrees in Philosophy (B.Phil., 2009) and Theology (B.S.T., 2014) from the Pontifical Urban University, Rome, and following priestly ordination in 2015, Father Amadi came to Notre Dame in 2017 earning a Master of Theological Studies degree (M.T.S., 2019) with concentration in Systematic Theology. His areas of interest include Liturgical and Sacramental Theology, Ethnography and Ritual Studies, Sociology and Anthropology of Christianity in Africa, and The Theology and Science of Gratitude.

His dissertation explores the religious imagination of contemporary African Christians through the ethnographic study of thanksgiving rituals in the Nigerian Catholic culture.
